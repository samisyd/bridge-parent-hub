### PAGE ROUTING & EDUCATIONAL ARCHITECTURE

Build the following specific navigation tabs/pages within the application, embedding the structured content explicitly into the UI templates and referencing "UniversityofMinnesota_ParentManual.pdf" verbatim when providing manual frameworks:

#### Page 1: Guide for Parents & Common Queries
- **Section 1: The 6 Steps to Effective Communication (From UniversityofMinnesota_ParentManual.pdf)**
  Render this as a clear visual stepper or vertical timeline for the parent to follow:
  1. **"I Care":** Build connection first to lower defensiveness (e.g., "I care about you and don't want you to get hurt").
  2. **"I See":** State objective facts without inserting opinions or blame ("When you came in last night, you were 3 hours late").
  3. **"I Feel":** Share your feelings objectively without accusatory blame ("I felt really scared and worried").
  4. **Listen:** Stop talking. Actively give them space to share their side, or offer to talk later if they aren't ready.
  5. **"I Want":** State clear expectations for next steps ("I want you to call me to let me know you're safe").
  6. **"I Will":** Define what you will or will not do to support the problem moving forward.
- **Section 2: Interactive Common Queries Accordion**
  Provide collapsible FAQ cards answering common parent dilemmas using positive parenting methodologies:
  - *Q: "My teen rolls their eyes and walks away whenever I try to talk. What do I do?"*
  - *Q: "How do I enforce a curfew without a screaming match?"*
  - *Q: "How do I balance giving them independence while monitoring their safety?"*

#### Page 2: Teenager Brain & Development

  1. *Pruning:* The "Use it or lose it" process starting at age 11 (girls) / 12 (boys) where unused neural pathways die out and reinforced pathways strengthen.

  2. *10 Minutes When You Reunite:* Reconnecting after school or work. Looking them in the eye, asking about their day, listening intently, and signaling "You matter, I missed you."
  3. *10 Minutes Before Bedtime:* Sacred wind-down time when teens naturally seek comfort or reassurance. Sharing affirmations, quiet reading, or a safe bedtime chat.
